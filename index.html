The problem

Markets don't wait
for you to catch up

Pre-market moves set the tone for the entire session. Most traders and analysts miss the signal in the noise.

No time before the open

Markets open at 9:30 AM. Synthesizing overnight futures, macro data, and individual symbols in time is near-impossible manually.

Signal buried in data

130 symbols, RSI readings, moving averages, sector rotation — the data exists but extracting actionable signal takes expert judgment.

History you can't search

Previous briefings hold context that shapes today's moves. Without a way to query that history, you start from zero every morning.

Features

Everything you need,
before the bell

Technical indicators at scale

RSI-14, 9/20/50-day moving averages, and 20-day sparklines computed for every watchlist symbol. Top-5 gainers and losers from a 71-symbol universe, automatically surfaced.

Gemini 2.5 Flash commentary

Every briefing is narrated by Gemini 2.5 Flash with a structured prompt, logged with latency and status. If the model call fails, a template briefing ships anyway — no missed mornings.

Polygon.io fallback ingestion

Primary data via yfinance with automatic fallback to Polygon.io for equities. Covers futures, macro indicators, sector ETFs, and your custom watchlist — 130 symbols without gaps.

Double opt-in email delivery

HTML briefing delivered via Resend (production) with double opt-in confirmation and per-address unsubscribe tokens baked in. GDPR-safe from day one.

How it works

Fully automated, every weekday

GitHub Actions triggers at 14:00 UTC. By the time US markets open, your briefing is already in your inbox.

01

Ingest

130 symbols pulled across futures, macro, sector ETFs, and watchlist via yfinance + Polygon.io fallback.

02

Compute

RSI-14, MA9/MA20/MA50, sparklines. Top-5 gainers and losers ranked from 71-symbol mover universe.

03

Narrate

Structured summary fed to Gemini 2.5 Flash. Every call logged with prompt, output, latency, and status.

04

Deliver

HTML briefing sent via Resend. Embedded into pgvector for full-history RAG Q&A queries.
